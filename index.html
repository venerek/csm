Submission Rules at a Glance
  • Title: Max 15 words
  • Abstract length: Max 3,125 characters (including spaces; excludes authors, institutions, references)
  • References (Poster): 5–7 most relevant bibliographic references
  • References (Platform): Must be from 2020 or later — missing references may result in rejection
  • Citations: In-text encouraged but not required (e.g., [1] or superscript¹)
  • Originality: No prior national presentation or publication
  • Presenting author: Max 2 abstracts as presenting author; unlimited as secondary
  • APTA membership: At least one author must be an active APTA member
  • Registration: Accepted authors must register for CSM — no fee waivers for presenters

📋 APTA Research Areas

Research Reports must select one key area from the APTA Research Agenda:

1. Population Health — Health disparities, social determinants, health promotion & prevention
2. Mechanistic — Biological/behavioral processes, pathophysiology, mechanism of action
3. Clinical — Diagnostic procedures, interventions, technology, factors affecting outcomes
4. Education & Professional Development — Learning processes, educational outcomes
5. Health Services — Financing, organizational structures, healthcare access, quality & costs
6. Workforce — Growth/attrition, provider demographics, practice environments, utilization
📌 Presentation Formats: Posters are displayed for 2 hours during a pre-assigned time. Platform presentations are 10–15 minute oral presentations.
Writing Tips by Section

✦ For All Abstract Types

  • Use clear, direct language — avoid jargon
  • Every sentence must earn its place
  • Read aloud to catch awkward phrasing

✦ Research Report Tips

  • Purpose: State research question & hypothesis in 1–2 sentences
  • Methods: Specify design, setting, participants, key measures
  • Results: Report actual data with statistics — be specific
  • Conclusions: Directly answer the hypothesis; don't overstate
  • Relevance: Answer "so what?" for practitioners

✦ Case Study Tips

  • Background & Purpose: Explain why this case is unusual, instructive, or clinically significant
  • Case Description: Include patient demographics, diagnosis, examination findings, and intervention details
  • Outcomes: Report objective measures — outcome tools, functional gains, discharge status
  • Discussion: Connect findings to existing literature; acknowledge limitations and lessons learned

✓ Quality Checklist

  • Does your title clearly reflect the content?
  • Have you addressed every required section?
  • Is clinical relevance or significance explicit?
  • Have you stayed within word/character limits?
  • Are all co-authors listed with correct affiliations?
  • Have you proofread for typos and grammar?
